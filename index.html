communities
Student-Initiated Courses (STICs) @ UMD is a program that helps students design, develop, and teach their own for-credit class at the university. I co-founded the program in the spring 2017 semester with Sashank Thupukari.
The program describes itself as "a community of individuals dedicated to building a brighter future for our industry and the Silicon Valley." I was fortunate to become part of the community during summer 2017 at Indiegogo.
The QUEST Honors Program is a hands-on program for UMD students to work on team-based, experiential learning projects. This program introduced me to some of the most motivated people I met at UMD.
Terrapin Hackers is UMD's hacker collective. The org has a hackerspace, runs hacktorials, hosts hack nights, and takes hackers to hackathons. I was the group's president during the spring 2016 semester.
Startup Shell is UMD's student-run startup incubator. It's an amazing community of individuals who are working on projects all over the globe. I joined the group in 2015 and have been an active member ever since.

💻 CMSC389K: Full-stack Web Development with Node.js
spring 2017, fall 2017
Sashank Thupukari and I designed, developed, and taught a 1-credit course in UMD's CS department on web development with Node.js. We taught it in the Spring 2017 and Fall 2017 semesters. In the following semesters, other students offerered to teach and the course has since expanded to two sections. The content is getting better and better, and will continue to grow!
👨🏽‍🏫IDEA498: Learning Experience Design Studio
fall 2018, spring 2019
Meenu Singh and I developed a course on learning experience design based on six levers of pedagogy: space, artifacts, activities, assessement, objectives, and culture. We wanted to teach Student-Initiated Course (STIC) facilitators how to intentionally design a classroom experience. In fall 2017, we taught this course to Tianxin Chen, Andres Arbelaez, and Nathalyn Nunoo. You can read Nathalyn's thoughts on teaching here. The next semester, Tianxin joined the teaching team, and we taught the course to 5 STICs facilitators.
🙇🏽‍♀️ CMSC389O: The Coding Interview
fall 2017
In University of Maryland’s CS department, there was a huge demand for software engineering internships. However, students felt like they needed more practice and time to prepare. My good friend Cameron Payton and I designed and taught CMSC389O: “The Coding Interview” in the fall 2017 semester to 28 students. The initial repository can be found here. Since, the class has expanded to over 3 sections, 7+ course facilitators, and 70+ students each semester.

hacks
🕹 human pong
april 2018
We projected a Javascript Pong Arcade Game onto the ground and had players stand as paddles on each side. We tracked them with OpenCV, and translated their movement into paddle movements realtime! I worked with Sashank Thupukari, Matthew Kaufer, Michael Stevens, and Cameron Payton on this project. Here is the link. We won “Best UX” at the hackathon.
☁️ text2download
may 2017
I was tired of listening to songs on SoundCloud and not having them in my personal library. I was generally listening to SoundCloud on the go, and so it was pretty difficult to remember to download songs when I was at a computer. So, I wrote a service that allowed me to text a number a SoundCloud link, and it would download the song directly to my Dropbox. The repo is found here (with a quick gif demo).
🍺 beer pong robot
april 2016
We wanted to create a robot that could play beer pong. We 3D printed the launching mechanism, which could adjust the launch speed and angle. We trained it using thin-plate splines, and it sunk a shot against the university president. I worked with Michael Stevens, Paul Lathrop, Shariq Hashme, and Ray Tann on this project. Here is a video.
🚗 vroom vroom
january 2016
"VRoom-VRoom is an immersive user experience that integrates virtual reality via an Oculus Rift. The user controls a miniature vehicle trapped inside a tabletop maze with the objective of finding the treasure hidden at the center of the labyrinth. With a first-person view through Rift, the user guides the vehicle by sending directions through a Myo armband. The user’s movements are translated into instructions for Spark Core which causes the car to move in the direction desired by the user." That description comes from Devpost. I worked with Geena Gao, Anthony Castrio, Ibrahim Hashme, and Shyam Patel on this project.
✈️ #dronepollock
november 2015
Stef Cohen and Nick Aversano recruited me to help with this art hack for Technica 2015. Hackers would navigate to a website with an interface to pick a spot on a canvas for a (real) drone to paint. We would get the coordinates, and controlled a (real) drone to paint on a (real) canvas in an art studio. Check out this article (with a video) Stef wrote. I worked with Stef, Nick, Josh Ermias, and Patrice-Morgan Ongoly on this project.

👬 Diversity & Inclusion Design Thinking Workshop
February 2019
I worked with Dr. Dean Chang to design a "Diversity & Inclusion Design Thinking Workshop." The workshop had about 15 participants. We asked participants to think about times they realized someone else's reality is fundamentally different from their own, and brainstorm how components of these experiences may be replicated in a university setting. For more information, please feel free to reach out (contact info below)!
🎓 Joint President/Senate Inclusion & Respect Task Force
2017 - 2018
From the website: "The Inclusion & Respect Task Force Force was created jointly by the President and the Senate to consider how best to nurture a climate that is more respectful and inclusive of all members of our campus community, stands against hate, and reaffirms the values that define us a University. More information about the task force's work can be found in Senate Document #17-18-03." Specifically, I worked on the university's free speech policies and counterspeech statement, and explored the opportunity of using Design Thinking workshops in relation to diversity/inclusion university initiatives.
